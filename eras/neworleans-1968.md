# Era Bible — New Orleans 1968 · "Vieux Carré" (draft — M5)

**Palette:** swamp green `#7fa06f` over near-black `#070a06`. **Type:** a slab (Zilla Slab), serif fallback.
**One line:** A vice cop on the take, a missing photographer, river humidity, café au lait at dawn as a dead drop.

## Tone and vocabulary

- Register: Chandler transplanted to the swamp — *evoked, never quoted*. First-person weariness, similes earned not stacked, corruption as climate. The heat here is literal and moral at once.
- Encouraged nouns: shotgun house, levee, darkroom, contact sheet, patrol log, hurricane glass, streetcar transfer, ceiling fan, chicory.
- Sound: brass leaking out of doorways, rain on galvanized roofs, the river's horn.

## Puzzle flavor mapping

- Ciphers: darkroom notations; classified-ad codes in the Picayune.
- Cross-file: patrol logs × pawnshop tickets × contact sheets.
- Interrogation: a bartender who saw nothing twice, a beat cop choosing a side, the photographer's sister.
- Dead drops: café saucer change, the third pew at St. Louis Cathedral, a crab trap off the levee.
- Photo analysis: the photographer's own last roll IS the case — reflections, timestamps, a badge number half-turned.

## Sensitivity notes

1968 New Orleans is a segregated city in a convulsing year. Race is context with weight, never costume or dialect minstrelsy. Police corruption is the noir engine; police violence is implication and aftermath, not spectacle.

## Failure-card styles

The file goes to a drawer that doesn't open. A transfer to a parish nobody can find on a map. The river keeps what it's given.
