# Era Bible — Paris 1954 · "The Blue Cellar" (draft — M5)

**Palette:** smoky blue `#7da7c7` over near-black `#06080a`. **Type:** a grotesque (Archivo), sans fallback.
**One line:** Saint-Germain jazz caves, a horn player with two passports, smoke you can taste.

## Tone and vocabulary

- Register: the Saint-Germain cellar scene of the Vian years — *evoked, never quoted*. Wit is allowed here; Berlin's dread relaxes into seduction and doubt. Everyone is performing; the question is for whom.
- Encouraged nouns: set list, reed case, cloakroom chit, carte d'identité, zinc counter, Gauloises haze, cellar stairs, last métro, hotel register.
- Sound: brushes on a snare, glassware, the pause between sets — intel moves *between* songs.

## Puzzle flavor mapping

- Ciphers: jazz set-list acrostics (first letters of tunes); chord-chart margins.
- Cross-file: hotel registers × ferry manifests × the second passport.
- Interrogation: musicians who answer in stories, a chanteuse who trades in favors, a barman who forgets faces for a price.
- Dead drops: the reed case at the coat check, a poster tube behind the zinc.
- Photo analysis: who is *not* applauding; a wristwatch on the wrong wrist.

## Sensitivity notes

Occupation memory is nine years old; collaboration accusations are live wounds and must be handled as such, not as flavor. Algerian tension is beginning — background pressure, never costume.

## Failure-card styles

Exposure, not violence: the wrong photograph published, an expulsion order, a residency card unrenewed. The cellar simply stops knowing your name.
